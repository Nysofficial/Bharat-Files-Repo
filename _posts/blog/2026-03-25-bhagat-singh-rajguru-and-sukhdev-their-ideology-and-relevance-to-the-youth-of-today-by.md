[Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Bhagat+Singh,+Rajguru,+and+Sukhdev&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350) hold a very important place in the [Indian freedom struggle](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Indian+freedom+struggle&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350),

not only because of their bravery and sacrifice but also because of their highly influential and

[progressive ideology](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=progressive+ideology+Indian+revolutionaries&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350). They were not only revolutionaries who fought against the British regime

through armed struggle, but also intellectual minds who had a vision for an equal and progressive

society. They inspire many, especially the youth, even today.

Bhagat Singh, being the most prominent among them, believed that revolution is not only about

violence and bloodshed. For him, revolution mainly meant bringing about a revolutionary change

in the society by challenging the status quo and arousing the masses. Through his writings, it is

evident that he had profound knowledge about politics, economics, and society. Under the

influence of [socialist ideology](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=socialist+ideology+Bhagat+Singh+Rajguru+Sukhdev&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350), he strongly opposed economic and [social inequality](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=social+inequality+India&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350). His one of the

essay, “Why I Am an Atheist," emphasized the need for [rational thinking](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=rational+thinking+Bhagat+Singh&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350), questioning, and

developing a scientific temper, which is highly relevant even today.

Rajguru and Sukhdev were as committed to this revolutionary idea. They had faith in collective

work and unity among the youth. Their involvement in revolutionary work was not for their own

glory, but they had a common cause for national freedom. All of them alwayd stood for discipline,

bravery, and dedication, which proved that change always requires both ideology and practice.

The ideology of these revolutionaries is of immense significance in today’s context. Today, India

is facing a lot of problems, ranging from unemployment, corruption, and social inequality to the

dissemination of false information. Under these circumstances, the ideology of Bhagat Singh,

Rajguru, and Sukhdev urges the youth to think and get involved in the democratic process. Their

emphasis on education and awareness proves that change requires a responsible and conscious

citizen.

Furthermore, their idea of freedom was not just [political freedom](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=political+freedom+India&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350). Their idea was to build a free

India, which would be [equal, just, and secular](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=vision+of+equal+just+and+secular+India&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350). Their commitment to these values urges the youth

today to rise above the narrow differences between castes, religions, and regions. Their

commitment to their cause proves that being a patriot is not just an emotional response, but a

continuous process to strive for change.

Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev all three were not only martyrs but also visionaries whose

ideas and ideologies are still highly relevant even today. Their ideology encourages the today’s

youth to be brave, disciplined, and be responsible citizens. By following their ideologies of justice,

equality, and rational thinking, today’s youth can contribute to the [nation building](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=nation+building+India&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=3894236397326835350) and ensure that

their legacy lives on.
