In January 2026, the long-standing conflict between the United States and Venezuela escalated into direct military action under the Trump administration. 

## Recent Major Developments (January 2026)

### Capture of Nicolás Maduro :

On January 3, 2026, U.S. special operations forces (Delta Force) conducted a large-scale strike in Caracas, capturing President Nicolás Maduro and his wife, Cilia Flores. They were flown to the U.S. and appeared in a New York federal court on January 5, pleading not guilty to narco-terrorism and cocaine trafficking charges.



### Casualties :

The operation, codenamed "[Operation Absolute Resolve](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Operation+Absolute+Resolve+Venezuela&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058)," resulted in significant casualties, including an estimated 23–24 Venezuelan soldiers and 32 members of the Cuban military and intelligence agencies.

### Current Leadership : 

Vice President [Delcy Rodríguez](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Delcy+Rodr%C3%ADguez+Venezuela&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058) was sworn in as interim president on January 5. While she denounced the capture as a "kidnapping," there are reports of her coordinating with U.S. officials to manage the post-Maduro transition.

### U.S. Intentions :

President Trump stated that the U.S. will "run" Venezuela until a proper transition occurs and expressed intent to have U.S. firms rebuild and develop Venezuela’s oil infrastructure. Secretary of State [Marco Rubio](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Marco+Rubio+Venezuela&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058) later clarified that the U.S. does not intend to govern the country but will maintain a military presence and an oil blockade to ensure compliance. 



## Background and Escalation (2025)

### The 2026 raid followed a months-long pressure campaign throughout 2025: 

### Naval Blockade and Strikes :

 Beginning in September 2025, the U.S. deployed a large naval fleet including the aircraft carrier [USS Gerald R. Ford](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=USS+Gerald+R.+Ford&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058) to the Caribbean. U.S. forces conducted over 35 strikes on alleged drug-trafficking boats, killing more than 100 people.

### Terrorist Designations : 

The U.S. labelled the [Cartel de los Soles](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Cartel+de+los+Soles&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058) (allegedly led by Maduro) and the Tren de Aragua gang as Foreign Terrorist Organizations (FTOs) to provide a legal basis for military force.

### Oil Conflict: 

In late 2025, the U.S. began seizing oil tankers accused of evading sanctions, aiming to cut off the Maduro government's primary source of revenue



## Core Disputes

### Narco-Terrorism : 

The U.S. justifies its actions by accusing the Maduro regime of orchestrating drug trafficking (fentanyl and cocaine) into America.

### Resource Control :

 Venezuela holds the world’s largest proven oil reserves. Maduro consistently accused the U.S. of using the "war on drugs" as a pretext to seize these resources.

### Migration : 

The Trump administration has blamed Maduro for the surge of Venezuelan migrants at the U.S. southern border, alleging he "emptied his prisons" to send criminals to the U.S..

### Democratic Legitimacy : 

The U.S. and many allies rejected the results of the 2024 Venezuelan presidential election as fraudulent, initially recognizing opposition candidate [Edmundo González](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Edmundo+Gonz%C3%A1lez+Venezuela&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058) as the legitimate winner. 





## Who is in Charge Now?

#### While the U.S. has captured Maduro, his party remains in control of the Venezuelan government for now:

### Interim President : 

Vice President Delcy Rodríguez was sworn in as acting president on January 5, 2026, following a Supreme Court order.

### The Power Dynamic : 

While Rodríguez has denounced the U.S. action as a "kidnapping," there are conflicting reports about her willingness to collaborate with Washington to stabilize the country's energy sector.

### The Opposition : 

Opposition leader [María Corina Machado](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Mar%C3%ADa+Corina+Machado+Venezuela&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058), who won the 2025 [Nobel Peace Prize](https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&q=Nobel+Peace+Prize&bbid=1796604895566559000&bpid=1871187579702328058), remains sidelined as the U.S. has focused on working with established military and government figures to avoid a total power vacuum.
